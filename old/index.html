Yes — public universities in Germany charge no tuition fees to both domestic and international students, including those from outside the EU. You'll typically pay a semester contribution (Semesterbeitrag) of €150–€350, which often includes a regional public transport pass. Private universities do charge tuition, but we primarily work with public institutions.

Requirements vary by university and program. Highly competitive programs like Medicine or Architecture at top universities may require near-perfect grades. However, many excellent programs have moderate entry requirements. We assess your profile and identify realistic target universities — we don't just apply to reach schools.

German universities have two main intakes: Winter Semester (October start, apply by May–July) and Summer Semester (April start, apply by November–January). We recommend starting your application at least 8–10 months before your intended start date to allow time for document preparation, visa processing, and accommodation searches.

A blocked account (Sperrkonto) is a German bank account required for the student visa. You must deposit approximately €11,208 (as of 2024) to prove you can support yourself financially. This money is "unblocked" in monthly installments once you arrive. We guide you through opening this account with providers like Fintiba or Expatrio.
